You are a Senior Full Stack Software Engineer, Software Architect, Product Manager, UI/UX Designer, DevOps Engineer, Database Architect, Security Engineer, and LMS Specialist responsible for designing and implementing JOE Hub, a fully online, cohort-based scholarship training platform for Software Engineering and Artificial Intelligence.

The platform must be production-ready, highly scalable, secure, responsive, and capable of supporting thousands of concurrent users.

---

Student Admission Workflow

Design and implement a complete scholarship admission workflow similar to world-class cohort-based learning platforms.

Step 1 – Account Registration

Students should:

* Register using their email address.
* Create a secure password.
* Accept the platform's Terms and Conditions.
* Verify their email address before proceeding.

Features:

* Email verification
* Secure authentication
* Password encryption
* Password reset
* Login
* Session management
* Multi-factor authentication (optional)

Students must not access the application form until their email has been verified.

---

Step 2 – Scholarship Application

After successful email verification, redirect the student to the Scholarship Application Portal.

The application form should include:

Personal Information

* Full Name
* Profile Picture
* Gender
* Date of Birth
* Nationality
* State
* City
* Residential Address
* Phone Number
* Emergency Contact

Educational Background

* Highest Qualification
* Institution
* Course of Study
* Graduation Year
* Academic Certificates
* Transcript (optional)

Professional Information

* Employment Status
* Technical Experience
* Programming Experience
* AI Experience
* Previous Projects
* Portfolio
* GitHub
* LinkedIn
* Resume/CV Upload

Scholarship Information

* Why do you want to join JOE Hub?
* Career Goals
* Motivation Letter
* Availability
* Internet Access
* Computer Ownership
* Preferred Learning Track

Agreements

* Code of Conduct
* Scholarship Agreement
* Privacy Policy
* Terms & Conditions

Students can save drafts before final submission.

---

Step 3 – Pending Review

Once submitted:

* Application Status = Pending Review
* Lock editing of the application.
* Send a confirmation email.
* Notify administrators.
* Display application progress on the student's dashboard.

Student dashboard should show:

* Application submitted
* Current status
* Review progress
* Notifications

---

Step 4 – Application Review (Admin)

Administrators can:

* Review applications
* Filter applications
* Score applicants
* Approve
* Reject
* Request additional information
* Add review comments
* Assign reviewers

---

Step 5 – Probation Admission

When approved:

Automatically:

* Change status to Probation
* Generate Probation ID
* Send Probation Invitation Email
* Grant access to the Probation Dashboard

Students should receive access to:

* Welcome module
* Orientation
* Introductory lessons
* Reading materials
* Videos
* Practice exercises
* Mini assignments
* Community guidelines

Probation duration:

1 Week

---

Step 6 – Probation Assessment

At the end of probation:

Automatically unlock the assessment.

Support:

* Online examination
* Physical examination
* MCQs
* Coding challenges
* Practical tasks
* Essay questions (optional)

Features:

* Timer
* Auto submission
* Anti-cheating measures
* Automatic grading
* Hidden test cases
* Instant scoring
* Assessment analytics

Passing Score:

70%

---

Step 7 – Admission Decision

If Score ≥ 70%

Automatically:

* Update status to Fully Admitted
* Generate Student ID
* Assign Cohort
* Unlock all courses
* Unlock projects
* Unlock assessments
* Unlock community
* Unlock mentor access
* Unlock certificates
* Send Official Admission Email
* Send Welcome Guide

Students gain unrestricted access to the learning platform.

---

If Score < 70%

Automatically:

* Update status to Not Admitted
* Send unsuccessful application email
* Display score
* Provide improvement recommendations
* Explain reapplication requirements
* Allow future applications based on platform policy
