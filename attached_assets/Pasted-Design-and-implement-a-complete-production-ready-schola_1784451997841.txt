Design and implement a complete, production-ready scholarship admission system for JOE Hub, a fully online, cohort-based Software Engineering and AI training platform.

The admission process should be fully automated, secure, scalable, responsive, and designed to provide a seamless experience from registration to final admission.

Step 1 – Account Registration

Allow prospective students to create an account using:

* Email address
* Secure password
* Password confirmation

During registration:

* Validate all inputs.
* Prevent duplicate accounts.
* Require acceptance of the Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policy.
* Send an email verification link immediately after registration.

Students must verify their email address before proceeding. Until verification is complete, they should not have access to the scholarship application form or any learning content.

---

Step 2 – Email Verification

After clicking the verification link:

* Mark the email as verified.
* Activate the student's account.
* Automatically redirect the student to the Scholarship Application Portal.

If verification fails or the link expires:

* Allow the student to request a new verification email.

---

Step 3 – Scholarship Application Form

After email verification, require every student to complete a comprehensive scholarship application form before continuing.

The form should include:

Personal Information

* Full name
* Profile photo
* Gender
* Date of birth
* Nationality
* State/Province
* City
* Residential address
* Phone number
* Emergency contact

Educational Information

* Highest qualification
* Institution attended
* Course of study
* Graduation year
* Academic certificates
* Transcript (optional)

Technical Background

* Programming experience
* AI experience
* Technical skills
* Previous projects
* GitHub profile
* LinkedIn profile
* Portfolio website
* Resume/CV upload

Scholarship Questions

* Why do you want to join JOE Hub?
* Career goals
* Motivation statement
* Availability
* Internet access
* Computer ownership
* Preferred learning track

Agreements

* Code of Conduct
* Scholarship Agreement
* Privacy Policy
* Terms & Conditions

Students should be able to save drafts before final submission.

---

Step 4 – Application Submission

When the application is submitted:

Automatically:

* Lock the application from further editing.
* Change the application status to Pending Review.
* Generate a unique application ID.
* Record the submission date and time.
* Send a confirmation email acknowledging receipt of the application.
* Notify administrators that a new application has been submitted.

The student's dashboard should clearly display:

* Application ID
* Submission date
* Current application status
* Review progress

---

Step 5 – Application Review

Administrators should be able to:

* Review applications.
* Filter and search applicants.
* Score applicants.
* Approve or reject applications.
* Request additional information.
* Leave internal review comments.
* Track review history.

---

Step 6 – Probation Invitation

If an application is approved:

Automatically:

* Update the student's status to Probation.
* Generate a Probation ID.
* Send a Probation Invitation Email.
* Grant access to the Probation Dashboard.

The probation period should last one week.

During probation, students should only have access to:

* Welcome and orientation modules
* Introductory learning resources
* Beginner assignments
* Practice exercises
* Community guidelines
* Limited platform features

They must not have access to the full training platform.

---

Step 7 – Probation Assessment

At the end of the one-week probation period:

Automatically unlock the assessment.

The assessment may be:

* Online
* Physical (configured by administrators)

The system should support:

* Multiple-choice questions
* Coding assessments
* Practical exercises
* Timed examinations
* Automatic submission
* Automatic grading
* Hidden test cases (for coding assessments)
* Anti-cheating measures
* Instant score calculation

The minimum passing score is 70%.

---

Step 8 – Final Admission

If the student scores 70% or above:

Automatically:

* Update account status to Fully Admitted.
* Generate a Student ID.
* Assign the student to their cohort.
* Unlock the complete learning platform.
* Grant access to all courses, learning resources, coding workspaces, projects, assessments, mentor support, discussions, and community features.
* Send an Official Admission Email.
* Send onboarding instructions and a welcome guide.

---

Step 9 – Unsuccessful Applicants

If the student scores below 70%:

Automatically:

* Update account status to Not Admitted.
* Send a polite notification explaining that they were not successful.
* Display the final assessment score.
* Provide constructive feedback where applicable.
* Explain the reapplication process and eligibility for future cohorts.

---

General Requirements

The entire admission workflow should be:

* Fully automated where appropriate.
* Secure and production-ready.
* Role-based and permission-controlled.
* Scalable for thousands of applicants.
* Responsive across all devices.
* Auditable with complete status history and activity logs.
* Integrated with email notifications at every major stage.
* Designed to deliver a smooth, transparent, and professional scholarship admission experience from registration through final admission.
